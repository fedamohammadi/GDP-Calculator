Gross Domestic Product (GDP) measures the total economic output of a country within a year. It consists of the following components:

  • Consumption (C): Total spending by households.
  • Investment (I): Expenditure on goods used for future production.
  • Government Spending (G): Expenditure by the government on goods and services.
  • Net Exports (NX): Exports minus imports.
  • Imports (M): Goods and services purchased from other countries.
